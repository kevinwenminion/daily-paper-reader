Foundation machine-learning interatomic potentials (MLIPs) are rapidly replacing density-functional theory (DFT) for modelling structure and nuclear dynamics, making their fidelity in strongly correlated systems an urgent question. We test three foundation potentials on the low-temperature order of three correlated, isostructural ABO 3 perovskite oxides — LaMnO 3

(d4), LaCoO 3 (d6), and NdNiO 3 (d7) — running molecular dynamics for 1 ns on 80- and 160-atom supercells from 50 to 300 K with no system-specific training. The three oxides expose three distinct classes of low-temperature order that pose a hierarchy of difficulty for the po-tentials. The scalar class (NdNiO 3) has a simple geometric fingerprint and is captured. The 

vector class (LaMnO 3) requires knowing which Cartesian axis carries the long bond at each site, and is captured in magnitude but not in symmetry. The on-site class (LaCoO 3) — the 

1arXiv:2607.08351v1 [cond-mat.mtrl-sci] 9 Jul 2026 low-spin to high-spin crossover — is a purely site-local multiplet population shift with no spatial order parameter, and is inaccessible to present-day MLIPs. 

Foundation machine-learning interatomic potentials (MLIPs) trained on Materials Project total energies, forces and stresses —most notably MACE 1, an equivariant graph-neural-network foundation model 2, and the magnetism-supervised CHGNet 3—can now prosecute molecular dy-namics simulations to nanosecond timescales in arbitrary inorganic crystals, without any system-specific training. This has opened a route to finite-temperature structural ensembles in correlated transition-metal oxides that was previously confined to ab initio molecular dynamics that are lim-ited to much shorter time scales 4–6 . However, present-day foundation MLIPs are trained on Born– Oppenheimer total energies of charge- and spin-converged DFT with atomic number and local bonding environment as the primary descriptors; they have no direct access to local multiplets, ligand-hole physics, or orbital occupation. For which correlated electronic channels is the skeletal structural information sufficient, and for which is it not? We address this question with three isovalent A3+ B3+ O3 perovskites with nearly identical A-site ionic radii 7 but three textbook-different correlated B-site ground states. LaCoO 3 (d6, R¯3c at all temperatures T ) hosts a low-spin ground state with thermal population of intermediate- and high-spin multiplets above ∼35 K 8–12 . The spin-state crossover is strictly site-local and does not break symmetry. NdNiO 3 (d7, Pbnm above ∼200 K) undergoes a metal–insulator transition accompanied by a rocksalt breathing-mode distortion that establishes two crystallographically inequivalent Ni 2sites 4, 13–18 . Here the breathing mode is the structural enabler of an underlying bond/ligand-hole disproportionation. Finally, LaMnO 3 (d4, Pbnm ) freezes its cooperative Jahn–Teller (JT) phonon below TOO ≈ 750 K into nearest neighbor bonds with three different lengths, 1.91/1.97/2.18 ˚ A. The long axis alternates in the ab plane in C-type order 19, 20 —the order parameter and the struc-tural distortion are locked together. These three materials thus probe three different relationships between correlated electrons and the lattice: LCO: electronic order parameter with no structural signature; LMO: coupled electronic–structural order-parameter; NNO: structural order parameter as the enabler for metal-insulator transition 4.

Protocol. For each material we build an 80-atom supercell (16 B sites) in the experimentally established space group— R¯3c for LaCoO 3, Pbnm for NdNiO 3 and LaMnO 3. Any breathing or JT pattern emerging from MD is therefore generated by the dynamics, not imposed by the start-ing symmetry. All three runs use identical NVT molecular dynamics with the production MACE 

matpes r2scan head 1 via janus-core 21 (Nos´ e–Hoover chain thermostat, chain length 3, τT =100 fs; 1 fs timestep, 1 ns production after ≥50 ps equilibration, ≥5000 frames per T for T ∈{50 , 100 , 150 , 200 , 250 , 300 } K) and an identical structural descriptor pipeline (per-B-site bond statistics over the 6 nearest oxygen neighbours, plus dimensionless ordering parameters defined in Methods). This fixes every nuclear coordinate but does not take the dn electron occupation into account. As a finite-size check, we repeated all nine runs — the three materials with each of the three potentials (MACE with the matpes r2scan and omol heads, and CHGNet) — on doubled 160-atom (32 B-site) supercells at 200 K, under a matched protocol: 50 ps NPT equilibra-3tion with a diagonal cell-vector mask (independent a, b, c axes, no shear; Martyna–Tobias–Klein barostat, τP = 1 ps) ramped to the target T , followed by 1 ns NVT production at the equilibrated cell. Every classifier in this paper is reproduced at the 160-atom size, with each (material, po-tential) cell remaining on the same side of every threshold used below: Sarle’s Bimodality Co-efficient (BC) agrees to within 0.03, the rocksalt order parameter QRS (Methods) to within 0.05 (the omol –NdNiO 3 saturation QRS = −1.000 is preserved exactly), |Qstatic 2 | to within 0.05 ˚ A(the CHGNet–LaMnO 3 static ferro-orbital JT is retained, |Qstatic 2 | = 0 .25 ˚A at 200 K), and the ferro-orbital versus C-type orbital-order classification is unchanged in every case (Supplementary Section S6, Table S3). Supercell aliasing is therefore not the origin of any of the three failure modes.

The aggregated rocksalt order parameter QRS (Methods, Eq. 1) sits at −0.27 at 50 K and −0.20 at 300 K, and the per-Ni bond-mean distribution is sub-Gaussian at all T (excess kurtosis γ2 = −0.12 → − 0.20 ). Finally the per-Co bond-mean distribution is super-Gaussian with γ2 growing from +0 .03 to +0 .24 —a single narrowing environment. The LaCoO 3 spin-state crossover has no static real-space order parameter, so there is no structural footprint to reproduce. NdNiO 3: omol produces a fully sign-correlated rocksalt pattern at every T (QRS = −1.000 ,bimodality coefficient BC = 0 .81 –0.97 , where 5/9 is the threshold for bimodality 22 ). This is the structural fingerprint of the experimental low-T P21/n breathing-mode phase, reproduced from a Pbnm starting cell without system-specific input. matpes r2scan captures the same chan-nel as a precursor (QRS ≈ − 0.22 , Gaussian, BC = 0 .35 ); CHGNet captures it more weakly still (QRS ≈ − 0.15 , Gaussian) despite training on the +U scheme conventionally invoked to stabilise the nickelate breathing mode. The flat T -independent QRS = −1.000 of omol is the signature of a single breathing-basin MD trajectory, not a thermodynamic prediction of TMI ; we do not extract 

TMI from these constant-T runs. 

LaMnO 3: no potential reaches the experimental C-type antiferro-orbital pattern, but each fails in a different DFT-allowed direction. omol develops a partial-static JT distortion at 50 K 6only ( |Qstatic 2 | = 0 .152 ˚A, MFO = 0 .67 ), sustaining a per-frame dynamical Qabs ∼ 0.18 ˚A at all 

T . CHGNet finds a strong static distortion of nearly experimental magnitude at 50 K ( |Qstatic 2 | =0.42 ˚A) with all 16 Mn long-axes pointing the same Cartesian way ( MFO = 1 .0, MOO = 0 –0.25): a ferro-orbital (FO) pattern, not the experimental C-type. Structural-footprint criterion. The cross-MLIP outcome (Table 2; three-axis classification of electron–lattice coupling in Supplementary Section S3, Table S1) maps cleanly onto a single prin-ciple: a foundation MLIP trained on charge- and spin-converged DFT total energies will reliably reproduce the static low-temperature structural fingerprints of a correlated oxide if and only if the underlying electronic instability has condensed its order parameter onto a static lattice distor-

7tion that is already present in the training-set ground-state relaxations. The three test materials map onto this criterion as: LCO (no order parameter → no footprint → MACE matpes r2scan 

correctly reproduces the absence of footprint), NNO (electronic instability with breathing-mode structural enabler → matpes r2scan sees the precursor, omol sees the frozen pattern), LMO (orbital-degeneracy JT structural order parameter → all three potentials fail at the symmetry chan-nel even when they capture the magnitude). The criterion is sharper than “MLIPs cannot do corre-lated electrons”: it identifies the LCO multiplet-crossover channel as fundamentally unreachable from any structure-only model (the order parameter is not geometric), and the LMO C-type AFO channel as requiring training-set breaking of the long-axis degeneracy in the right symmetry pat-tern. This criterion lets us make falsifiable, prospective predictions: a foundation MLIP will (i) reliably capture the breathing-mode structural fingerprints of the wider RNiO 3 family insofar as the NdNiO 3 precursor result generalises across R 23–25 ; (ii) miss the entire LaCoO 3, Sr 2CoO x, and Co-spinel multiplet-crossover family unless explicitly fine-tuned on multiplet-resolved energetics; (iii) require the right potential (training-set proximity to the right JT-active subspace) to access the cooperative-Jahn–Teller channel in d4 manganates 26 , vanadates, and other e1

Each of these is testable on existing foundation potentials against established experimental structure data. 8Hierarchy of broken-symmetry channels. The three failure modes order naturally along a single axis of architectural difficulty. NdNiO 3 hosts a scalar collective bond mode (alternat-ing ± deviation of ⟨dNi −O⟩i on a rocksalt sublattice): a single number per site, learnable from nearest-neighbour bond-length anti-correlation alone. The molecular-trained omol head, with no exposure to extended-solid charge order, nonetheless locks the pattern in at QRS = −1.000 ;

matpes r2scan and CHGNet reach the precursor regime ( QRS ∈ [−0.15 , −0.22] ). For this class, adding a per-Ni spin label as an input variable would concomitantly resolve the metal– insulator transition and the spin-disproportionated S=0 /S =1 ground state, because the breathing geometry already encodes which sublattice is which: the structural and electronic order parameters are coupled and the structural one is already a single-coordinate function. LaMnO 3 requires one rank higher: the cooperative JT order parameter is a vector (director) field — which Cartesian axis is the long bond, alternating ⊥ between NN Mn. This selects the eg

orbital occupation per site, and the choice has no geometric prior in any training distribution that has not seen extended JT compounds with the correct C-type long-axis pattern. CHGNet, whose MPtrj-with-+U -and-magnetism supervision baked in a JT-distorted Mn 3+ ground state at training time, captures the magnitude ( |Qstatic 2 | = 0 .42 ˚A at 50 K, matching the experimental 0.39 ˚A) but lands on the wrong long-axis pattern (FO, not C-type AFO). LaCoO 3 is the genuinely hardest case: the spin crossover has no spatial order parameter at all, only a uniform ∼0.05 ˚ A bond elongation as Co 3+ populations shift LS →HS and a bond-mean distribution that broadens as the population becomes bimodal in spin space (the MACE omol 

9head shows precisely this mild leptokurtic enhancement). No enrichment of bond-based geometric features can recover it; a per-site spin population must enter as an explicit input variable. The three perovskites therefore traverse a hierarchy of architectural cost — scalar (NNO) → vector 

(LMO) → on-site population (LCO) — that any foundation MLIP aiming to handle correlated transition-metal oxides will have to climb in the same order. 

Outlook. The hierarchy makes the corrective training strategy concrete and material-specific. For the scalar (NNO) class, no new input variable is strictly required to fix the structural channel; geometry-only foundation MLIPs already reproduce the breathing footprint, and adding a per-site spin label upgrades that geometric agreement to the full concomitant metal–insulator + spin-disproportionation transition. For the vector (LMO) class, including explicit JT-active reference structures with the correct C-type long-axis pattern in the training set should break the FO/ C-type near-degeneracy; this is structurally well-defined and reachable with existing DFT+ U calculations. For the on-site (LCO) class, the order parameter does not exist as a static structural distortion; an explicit multiplet-resolved auxiliary input or target (LS/IS/HS population, local-spin-moment) is mandatory, and would need to be trained against multi-reference electronic-structure methods such as dynamical mean-field theory 27 or vertex-corrected extensions of quasiparticle self-consistent 

GW 28–31 that resolve the multiplet splittings.
